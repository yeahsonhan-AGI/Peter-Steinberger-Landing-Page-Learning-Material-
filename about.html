About

Peter Steinberger is an Austrian developer and entrepreneur, born and raised in Oberösterreich (Upper Austria). Unlike many in tech, he chose to remain in Austria throughout his career, building global companies from Vienna rather than relocating to Silicon Valley.

Education

He completed his secondary education at HTL Braunau (Higher Technical Institute) before studying engineering and computer science at Technische Universität Wien (Technical University of Vienna).

Philosophy

Peter is known for his pragmatic approach to development. In his own words: "I ship code I don't read" — reflecting his workflow centered around AI assistance to build and ship like a full team.

He believes in bootstrapping over venture capital, having built PSPDFKit from his apartment without external funding. This approach allowed him to maintain full control while growing the company to 60+ employees over 13 years before a successful exit.

Current Work

After experiencing what he described as a "deep emptiness" following his PSPDFKit exit, Peter turned his attention to artificial intelligence. He created OpenClaw, an open-source AI agent framework designed to run locally on users' devices for privacy.

This work led to his joining OpenAI, where he now works on next-generation personal agents — bringing his vision of private, local AI assistance to the forefront of the industry.

OpenClaw

After selling PSPDFKit, Peter experienced what he described as a "deep emptiness." Rather than retire, he turned his attention to the emerging field of artificial intelligence.

The result was OpenClaw (originally called ClawdBot) — an open-source AI agent framework designed with a singular focus: privacy.

Local-First Philosophy

Unlike most AI assistants that send data to cloud servers, OpenClaw was designed to run locally on users' devices. This architecture ensures that conversations and data never leave the user's control.

The framework supports multiple AI models and can integrate with tools and services, all while maintaining local processing for sensitive operations.

Features

OpenClaw enables users to run AI assistants that can connect to messaging platforms like WhatsApp and Slack, all hosted on their own infrastructure. Key capabilities include:

  • Multi-model support for flexibility
  • Tool integration for complex tasks
  • Local execution for privacy
  • Open-source transparency

Trademark Challenge

The project originally launched as "ClawdBot" but faced trademark issues with Anthropic over the "Claw" name — a reflection of how crowded and contested the AI landscape has become. The rebrand to OpenClaw positioned the project more clearly in the open-source community.

Legacy

OpenClaw's local-first approach to AI agents directly informed Peter's vision for his work at OpenAI, where he now brings this philosophy of privacy-focused, personal AI to the next generation of products.
